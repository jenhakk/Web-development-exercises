Homemade naan bread is so good it just melts in your mouth!

This fast version of naan bread is really easy to make in the home kitchen.

The genuine naan breads are traditionally baked in a very hot tandoor oven.
But since very few people have that at home, homebakers can use normal oven instead.

Naan bread

  • 2 dl water

  • 2 dl turkish yoghurt

  • 1 bag of yeast

  • 1 tbsp honey

  • 1 tsp salt

  • 6-7 dl flour

  • 2 tbsp oil

On top:

  • water

  • sesame seeds

After oven:

  • 50 g melted butter

  • 3 pcs garlic cloves

  • herbs, salt

  1. Mix the yeast with about 3 dl of flours. Add salt and honey with warm water (42 degrees).
    Add the flours with yeast in to the water. Add room temperature yoghurt and keep adding the flours.

  2. Leave the dough rest under a cloth for at least 30 minutes.

  3. Set the oven at 250 degrees.

  4. Add the oil in to the dough and keep kneading the dough. Divide the dough into 4 or 8 balls.
    Flatten them with your hand and put them in to a baking tray.
    Let them rest for 15 minutes.

  5. Sprinkle water and seeds on top of the breads. Bake about 5 minutes in the middle section of the oven.

  6. Melt the butter and grate the garlic cloves with it.
    You can also add fresh herbs in to the mix.

  7. Spread the breads with butter mix and enjoy!

Computer applications, Bachelor of business administration

What will you learn?
Programming requires logical thinking, understanding users and patience. Creating large applications is always teamwork.

Studies provide you with skills you can apply to many different programming jobs all over the world.

What will you become?

As you graduate from the Degree Programme in Computer Applications, you have studied many topics widely requested in IT industry.
Your title could be for example: Software and Application Developer, Digital Business Analyst, Systems Developer, Programmer,
